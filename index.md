## About Me

Welcome! I am a PhD student in the [CAMARADES](https://www.ed.ac.uk/clinical-brain-sciences/research/camarades) Research Group at the [University of Edinburgh](https://www.ed.ac.uk/).

I specialise in the meta-research of preclinical data. I am currently supervised by [Dr Emily Sena](https://www.ed.ac.uk/profile/dr-emily-sena) and [Prof Peter Kind](https://www.ed.ac.uk/discovery-brain-sciences/our-staff/research-groups/peter-kind). My PhD project is funded by the Simons Initiative for the Developing Brain.

I am passionate about open and reproducible research, and enjoy engaging the public with my research.

## Education

- **PhD**, *The University of Edinburgh*, Oct 2021 - (expected) Mar 2025
  <br>
  - Thesis: Developing a meta-research framework for preclinical models of autism, supervised by Dr Emily Sena, Prof Peter Kind.

- **B.Sc. in Neuroscience (Hons.), 2:1**, *The University of Edinburgh*, Sep 2015 - May 2019
  <br>
  - Dissertation: Systematic review of the efficacy of NMDA receptor antagonists in the in vitro PC-12 oxygen-glucose deprivation stroke model, supervised by Dr Emily Sena.
